SINDHUJA MADABUSHI

I am a PhD student in the Computer Science department at Virginia Tech advised by Jin-Hee Cho. My research focuses on privacy, security, fairness, and incentives in federated learning to enable collaborative machine learning while safeguarding individuals' data.

Previously, I conducted research at University of Wisconsin-Madison where I worked with Parmeswaran Ramanathan to design privacy-preserving algorithms for human genome read alignment. I received my Master's degree in Data and Knowledge Engineering from Otto-von-Guericke Universit√§t Magdeburg, and my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science from GITAM University.

My research interests encompass privacy and robustness in federated learning, design of privacy-preserving algorithms and incentive mechanisms, explainable AI, and applied cryptography. Through my work on federated learning and the design of incentive mechanisms, I aim to address critical challenges, ensuring that these systems are not only effective and efficient but also equitable and trustworthy.

Feel free to email me at msindhuja@vt.edu to discuss potential collaborations!
